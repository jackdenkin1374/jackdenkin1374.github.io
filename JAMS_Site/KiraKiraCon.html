There are 4 main attractions for this convention:

  • Dealer's & Artist's Room
    • Here attendees can support over 40 to 50 local artists and vendors.

  • Game Room
    • A room with a wide array of game consoles with running games and a VR headset setup.
    • There is also a local arcade cabinet rental with around 4 cabinets for 2020.

  • Panels
    • Over 15 panels throughout the day of various topics such as:
      • Cosplay
      • Seasonal Anime
      • Crafts
      • Fandoms
    • Name that Anime Tune.
    • DnD Oneshot campaign.
    • Karoke.

  • Cosplay Cafe
    • A cafe where waiters and waitresses serve Japanese style, homemade snacks and drinks.

Running a Convention

There is a lot that goes into running a convention from choosing a location that will allow a convention to run there, emailing local or out of state artists and vendors to come to the event and running the event for the entire duration from opening to closing.

  • Budget & Profit
  • Locations
  • Emails
  • Volunteers & Staffs
  • Schedules

Running a convention will cost a lot of money to start it and maintain it. Your organization may not even see a large margin of profit either for a long while depending on the event attendee numbers, artists & vendors profit cut and much more.
There are a lot of expenses and income the event will have.

  • Expense
    • Location Rental
    • Hiring Staffs
    • Game room equipment rental
    • Badges
    • Promotion
    • Guests' fees

  • Income
    • Badges sold
    • A percentage take on artists & dealers for table space
    • Other areas
      • Cafe

The locations that may allow such conventions are hotels with meeting rooms or large convention centers with available hotels nearby.
Depending on how many rooms and days the event needs, it will be the largest expense in the budget, which will cost thousands.

Once you managed to booked an location for the event, you will then have to annouce when and where your event is happening.
And then email lots of artists and vendors to come attend your event or handle incoming emails of people wanting to attend the event.
As well as handling emails for panelists and guests for the event to make it more entertaining and lively.

Hopefully you also have other adminstration staffs helping you in running this event, as you will need a lot of people to handle:

  • Panels transitions
  • At the door badge purchases
  • Security
  • Tournments
  • Maintaining other venues of interest

Once the event draws near and you have a large list of panels, artists & vendors and staffs for the event. You will have to plan out various maps and schedules for all of this.
Some key maps and schedules are:

  • Building maps of what room are what
    • Panel rooms
    • Artists & Dealers rooms
      • A map for this room as well for artists and vendors to share online to promote the event and let their followers know where they are in the event.
    • Game room
    • Bathrooms
    • Other interests

  • Panel Schedules
    • Which panel in which room
    • How long it is
    • Be sure not to have certain popular panels conflict with each other.

  • Staff Schedules
    • Let's not overwork people and have breaks.
    • Who will be where and what will they be doing.
    • Security locations and routes

  • Game Tournments
  • And many others
